# Homepage ClickUp-Inspired Subtle Polish

## Scope

This story is only for the public home page at `/`.

Do not change sign-in, onboarding, owner dashboard, customer pages, or any other route.

## Product posture

Bizing is the system a business grows into.

It is not presented as a booking app.
It is not presented as a workflow gimmick.
It should feel calm, clear, precise, and quietly powerful.

## Audience

- Primary audience: a business owner arriving for the first time
- Secondary audience: a returning business owner deciding whether to sign in

Nothing in this page should feel internal, experimental, or technical.

## Existing direction to preserve

- Black and white
- Clean sans-serif typography
- Premium business feel
- Calm, polished, matter-of-fact
- No jargon
- No internal words
- No suspicious trust/security language
- No loud gradient backgrounds
- No flashy visual effects
- No redesign of the page structure

## Existing structure to preserve

The current page already has the right information architecture.

Keep:

- the top-left Bizing logo
- the top-right sign-in button that appears on scroll
- the hero with three short lines
- the primary CTA and secondary sign-in text
- the `Built to grow with you` section
- the `Operations stay connected` section
- the `Automation stays in control` section
- the bottom CTA with the Bizing icon

Do not add new sections.
Do not remove sections.
Do not turn this into a redesign.

## User request for this pass

Take subtle inspiration from the feel of ClickUp's public marketing pages, especially:

- more refined text treatment
- a very subtle gradient inside key text, not a visible rainbow effect
- smoother, quieter transitions
- better hover and focus behavior
- slightly more polished cards and section surfaces
- slightly better rhythm and readability

The key word is subtle.

This should feel like a quiet professional polish pass on the existing Bizing page, not a new visual identity.

## Things to improve

- The hero headline can feel a little flatter than it should.
- The current text hierarchy is good but can feel more premium with restrained text treatment.
- Section cards and surfaces can feel slightly more refined without becoming soft or generic.
- Hover transitions should feel smoother and more deliberate.
- The fixed header sign-in reveal can feel a touch more polished.
- The bottom CTA section can feel a little more intentionally finished.

## Hard constraints

- Keep the same overall layout and copy intent.
- Keep the page mostly black and white.
- Any gradient must be extremely subtle and used only where it adds polish.
- Avoid obvious SaaS gloss.
- Avoid decorative glows, blobs, large shadows, or colorful backgrounds.
- Motion should be very subtle and short.
- Changes should make the page feel better, not different.

## UX goal

The page should feel a little more alive and a little more premium.

Sarah should feel:

- this is polished
- this is modern
- this is confident
- this is usable

without ever feeling:

- marketed to
- dazzled
- overwhelmed
- distracted

Kimi should propose an implementation-ready polish pass for the current home page.

Focus on:

- headline text treatment
- section/card refinement
- hover/focus/motion polish
- spacing and rhythm
- subtle visual continuity between sections

Do not rewrite the whole page.
